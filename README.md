# NPJ Phase

This is a CMEC-compliant module that evaluates the daily and monthly variability of the North Pacific jet stream during the cool season within gridded model datasets.

## Introduction

The variability of the North Pacific jet stream during the cool season (September–May) is defined by using EOF analysis to define the two leading modes of variability of upper-tropospheric (200, 250, or 300-hPa) zonal wind anomalies over the North Pacific during the cool season on daily or monthly timescales.
